Where It All Started

I grew up eating Chilaquiles, rojo or verde, for breakfast, lunch, and even dinner. They were comfort and deliciousness wrapped up in a divine meal. Chilaquiles date back over a hundred years ago to the early Aztecs. The word chilaquiles, pronounced chee-lah-kee-lehs, derives from the anciet Aztec Nahuatl language meaning "chilis and greens." Whenever I make this, I am transported to the small house on Balfour Street where I watched my nana prepare our food with love. Enjoy this recipe!

Ingredients:

  1. Cut up corn tortillas
  2. Red or green salsa
  3. Eggs *optional*
  4. Crema
  5. Cilantro
  6. Vegetable oil
  7. Chopped onion
  8. Cotijo queso
  9. Salt
  10. Choice of protien
  11. Sliced avocado *optional

How to Make Chilaquiles

  1. In a large frying pan, heat 2 tablespoons of oil over medium-high heat. Fry until the chips are golden brown.
  2. Cook chorizo for 3-4 minutes and let settle.
  3. In a saucepan over mediumheat, add 1 tablespoon of oil, chips and salsa of choosing.
  4. Meanwhile, in a seperate pan fry two eggs and for for one minute.
  5. Transfer corn tortillas, salsa and egg onto a plate. Cover in crema, cotija, onion, avocado and chorizo.

Make a fresh pot of coffee and enjoy!
